For a long time, forests were perceived as an economical source to generate income for their owners and were managed by national laws. Since 1980, a rising awareness for ecological problems and the worldwide decreasing forest coverage through deforestation lead to a public awareness of its consequences: air pollution, the decline in biodiversity and climate change. Since then, forest politics shifted from being a national to an international political issue.
The public discourse about tropical wood trade that leads to forest loss in the amazon rainforest started in the 1980s as well, with a consumer boycott of tropical wood that was called for by environmental organizations.
