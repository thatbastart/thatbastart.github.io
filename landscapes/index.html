Moneytree

Mahogany is the most valuable timber species in the Brazilian Amazon, worth up to four times the value of its nearest competitor. One cubic meter of first-quality sawn mahogany is worth about 1800 USD when it departs for America or Europe. A single large tree can produce more timber worth about 4100 USD if the wood is of high quality.


Who earns what?

Smallholders and indigenous peoples who sell mahogany trees to loggers rarely receive more than US$6-24/tree. If they receive any money at all! The mateiros (loggers) who scour the forest for mahogany, chainsaw operators who cut the trees, and haul the trees out of the forest to transport them to the nearest sawmill are also poorly paid.
