Distribution

Swietenia macrophylla has a large natural range, encompassing a vast territory from the southern part of Tamaulipas, Mexico, along the Atlantic coast through Belize, Guatemala, Nicaragua, Costa Rica and Panama, and on down into the northeastern part of South America, along the borderlands of the Amazon to Bolivia and the southern part of the Amazon in Brazil.

Due to its curved shape, we affectionately call the natural range of mahogany the whale of mahogany.

Countries

Mahogany is native to the following countries: Mexico, Belize, Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama, Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, and Brazil and it is an introduced species in commercial plantations in many regions of the world.
