Causes

Mahogany has an enormously high material quality. The wood is resistant to water and woodworm, and in addition, easy to process. Therefore, mahogany should initially replace walnut, which is native to Europe.

But the distinctive reddish-brown color and special grain, which makes the wood shimmer golden in the right light, made it a status symbol and precious wood.

Consequences

Rainforests are cleared for tropical timber. This is common knowledge. Mahogany is not exempt from this either. In the long run, the enormous exploitation leads to an extreme strain on the entire ecosystem.

The problem: mahogany trees grow extremely far apart. In order to get the coveted reddish-brown tropical wood, kilometer-long aisles must therefore be cut through the rainforest. Reforestation is often no longer possible here.
