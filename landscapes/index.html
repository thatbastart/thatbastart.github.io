Hypsipyla robusta

The biggest problem on mahogany plantation is actually a 30mm species: Hypsipyla robusta, commonly known as mahogany shoot borer. This insect can cause 100 percent mortality on seedlings and young plantations.

The caterpillars destroy the apical shoot, causing the tree to develop many lateral branches and often a deformed trunk, resulting in a reduction in the value of the wood.
There is no known viable method of controlling this pest.

Further Problems

Climate that does not correspond to native conditions and intensive mono cropping also leads to poorer quality of mahogany wood. Plantation mahogany is described as softer and more difficult to work with. It is also not as dense, the color is lighter, the grain is irregular and generally inferior.

Nevertheless, mahogany was for a long time the most sought-after tropical wood, from which enormous amounts of profit could be made.
