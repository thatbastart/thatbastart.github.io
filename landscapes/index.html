Not alone

There is no such thing as "the one and only mahogany tree". Like any other species, mahogany has been taxonomically classified. In fact, there exist three different species of tree which are regarded as "genuine mahogany”. Illustration: Vivien Serve

Swietenia mahagoni
© https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swietenia_mahagoni

Characteristics

They are all evergreen trees with a dense, dome-shaped, rounded but sometimes spreading crown. The most-known characteristic is their special timber: it has good durability, great dimensional stability, easy machining and high resistance to fungi and insects. These characteristics make mahogany technically one of the best woods in the world. Note that these texts are mostly talking about American mahogany.
